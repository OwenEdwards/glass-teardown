Gargoyles represent the embarrassing side of the Central Intelligence Corporation. Instead of using laptops, they wear their computers on their bodies, broken up into separate modules that hang on the waist, on the back, on the headset. They serve as human surveillance devices, recording everything that happens around them. Nothing looks stupider; these getups are the modern-day equivalent of the slide-rule scabbard or the calculator pouch on the belt, marking the user as belonging to a class that is at once above and far below human society.

Google's latest and hottest gadget needs no introduction. Since its public unveiling in April 2012, the tiny head-mounted Android computer has been adorning the faces of a diverse range of people, and has been widely accepted by the general public. Fortunately, they've made it relatively easy to obtain one--relative to the ease of obtaining a nuclear warhead, that is--which is why we're able to bring you the first detailed view into the electronics guts of the device.

The Receiving Ceremony

Glass is such a special device that typical physical shipment is not sufficient. Instead, you must schedule an in-person appointment at a "Glass Studio". At this appointment, a trained staff will walk you through the setup and usage of your Glass unit.
